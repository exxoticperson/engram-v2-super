# ENGRAM Battlecards

Use this skill to create sharp side-by-side comparisons that help sell ENGRAM.

## Best use cases

- prospect-specific prep before outreach or a call
- competitor framing in a sales deck
- "why ENGRAM" prep against Motion, Kantar, Neurons, or internal agency workflows
- local or category-specific comparisons between a target and three stronger peers

## What to produce

Every battle card should answer:

1. Who the target is
2. Who the three most relevant comparison points are
3. Where the target is weak
4. What the weakness likely costs them
5. Where ENGRAM fits into that gap

## Default structure

- target summary
- competitor set
- comparison table
- top three vulnerabilities
- ENGRAM angle
- one-line outreach hook

## Comparison dimensions

Use only the dimensions that matter for the case:

- pre-launch decision quality
- speed of creative iteration
- visibility into hook and reveal timing
- depth of diagnosis
- repair guidance
- category trend awareness
- ability to defend budget decisions to clients

## Rules

- keep it concrete, not theoretical
- avoid bloated SWOT-style filler
- if evidence is weak, label it as inferred
- translate findings into a sales angle, not just research notes

## Output style

Battle cards should feel commercially useful. The user should be able to take the result into a call or DM immediately.
