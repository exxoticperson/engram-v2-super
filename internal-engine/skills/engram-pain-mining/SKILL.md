# ENGRAM Pain Mining

Use this skill to turn raw public language into positioning fuel.

## Sources to mine

- agency founder posts
- LinkedIn comments
- Reddit threads
- X discussions
- reviews of creative tools
- interviews, podcasts, and public commentary

## What to look for

- repeated frustrations about creative testing
- fear of wasting media budget
- frustration with post-hoc tools
- inability to predict which ad to back
- client pressure around creative performance
- frustration with explaining creative decisions internally

## What to extract

For each source cluster:

- direct pain statement
- implied underlying fear
- buying trigger
- exact language worth reusing
- ENGRAM angle

## Output format

- pain theme
- who says it
- representative language
- why it matters
- how ENGRAM should respond

## Rules

- privilege real language over polished marketing language
- separate explicit complaints from inferred pain
- look for emotional stakes, not just surface complaints
- if Arabic or mixed-language text appears, preserve meaning in simple English without flattening the emotional tone

## Best downstream uses

- outreach personalization
- landing-page copy
- FAQ and objection handling
- offer reframing
- content hooks
