# ENGRAM LinkedIn Content Strategy

LinkedIn is ENGRAM's highest-leverage distribution channel. One strong post reaches thousands of the exact buyers you're cold messaging. This skill governs the content system.

---

## THE CONTENT STRATEGY IN ONE LINE

Demonstrate the product in public. Every post is a free partial audit.

Not: "Here's why pre-launch creative testing matters."
Yes: "I audited a [beauty] DTC ad and found exactly where it loses viewers at second 8. Here's the breakdown."

Show the work. The right people will DM you.

---

## POST TYPES (5 categories, rotate between them)

### Type 1 — Audit Excerpt (highest engagement)

Format: "I audited [description] ad. Here's what I found."

Structure:
- Hook: Specific, specific, specific. Not "a beauty ad" but "a [category] ad that was running at $2k/day"
- The diagnosis: One specific failure point, explained clearly
- The fix: What should change and why
- CTA: "If you want to see the full audit format, I'll send it."

**Template:**
```
I audited a [category] DTC ad running on [platform].

It had a beautiful visual hook. One problem: the viewer had no idea what they were watching for the first 9 seconds.

Here's what was happening:

The first frame was stunning — [describe visual]. Strong pattern interrupt. But there was no copy, no verbal hook, no reason to stay.

By second 7, our analysis showed a significant attention drop — the exact moment a viewer decides to scroll.

The fix was simple: add one line of copy in the first 2 seconds that names the problem the product solves. No reshoot. One edit.

That gap — between a strong visual and a missing semantic anchor — is what we call Hook Friction. It's the #1 failure pattern we see in [category] ads right now.

If you're running [category] creative and want to see what your ads look like through the same framework, reply or DM me.
```

---

### Type 2 — Category Trend Alert

Format: "What's working in [category] ads this week."

Structure:
- What's working (specific pattern)
- What's declining (specific pattern)
- What it means for anyone running creative in this space
- CTA: Soft — "Happy to run your current ads against this benchmark."

**Template:**
```
[Category] ads on TikTok this week — what's actually working:

Problem-first hooks: still winning. Ads that open with "if your [X] does Y, watch this" are holding attention through the 3-second mark at significantly higher rates than aesthetics-first opens.

What's declining: talking head with text flyins. Category is saturated. Engagement dropping vs 30 days ago.

Reveal timing: the sweet spot is product visible by second 5–6. Ads revealing after second 9 are losing audience before the value proposition lands.

If you're about to launch a [category] campaign, your creative is either aligned with these patterns or it isn't. The difference in hold rate is significant.

(These are patterns from our current audit data + weekly category tracking. Not a guarantee of outcomes — these are directional signals.)

What are you seeing in your creative testing? Curious if this matches.
```

---

### Type 3 — The Before/After (after first case study)

Format: "We audited [anonymized brand]. Here's the before/after."

Structure:
- The original ad's problem (specific)
- What we recommended (specific edit)
- What happened after implementation (if client shared data)
- CTA: Direct — "This is what ENGRAM delivers. Interested?"

Only use this format after you have a real case study. Don't fabricate.

---

### Type 4 — Competitor Contrast (careful, non-aggressive)

Format: "Most creative analytics tools tell you what happened. We tell you what's about to happen."

Structure:
- Name the category (creative analytics)
- Name the gap in existing solutions (post-hoc vs predictive)
- Position ENGRAM's difference without attacking by name
- CTA: Soft

**Template:**
```
There are great tools that tell you which of your ads performed best last month.

The gap: by the time you know, you've already spent the budget.

The question no tool fully answers before launch: "Where exactly will this ad lose the viewer, and what do I change?"

That's what we built ENGRAM to answer. Pre-launch. 48 hours. Specific to the second.

If you're making creative decisions based on last month's data, your creative will always be one cycle behind.

DM me if you want to see what pre-launch diagnostics look like on your current creative.
```

---

### Type 5 — Industry Data Drop

Format: "[X] things we learned from auditing [N] [category] ads."

Structure:
- 3–5 specific, numbered findings
- Each finding is a data point, not an opinion
- Source it ("from [N] audits in Q[X] 2026")
- CTA: Link to full quarterly report (when available) or offer to send

**Template:**
```
We analyzed [N] [category] DTC ads on TikTok and Meta this quarter.

Here's what the data showed:

1. [X]% of audited ads had a product reveal after the 8-second mark. In the current category environment, that timing is correlated with significantly lower predicted hold rates.

2. Hook Friction was the most common failure pattern — strong visual, weak semantic setup. [X]% of audits flagged it.

3. Ads with Modality Conflict (visuals, VO, and captions not reinforcing each other) scored an average of [X] points lower on ENGRAM's coherence dimension.

4. The highest-scoring ads had one thing in common: a problem-first hook that named the viewer's specific concern within 2 seconds.

5. [X]% of clients who implemented our recut recommendations reported improved hook rate on the revised creative.

Full Q[X] Creative Intelligence Report available on request. Reply or DM.
```

## POSTING FREQUENCY

**During validation sprint (Days 4–14):**
- 3 posts per week minimum
- Post on: Monday, Wednesday, Friday
- Alternate between Type 1 (audit excerpt) and Type 2 (trend alert)

**After first client:**
- 2 posts per week
- Mix Type 1, 2, and 3

**After quarterly report:**
- Spike to 5 posts in launch week
- Then back to 2/week

## HOOK FORMULAS (for every post)

The first line determines everything. Use one of these structures:

| Formula | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Specific number** | "I've now audited 23 [category] DTC ads." |
| **Counter-intuitive claim** | "Your best-looking ad might be your worst-performing one." |
| **The problem stated directly** | "Most [category] ads lose the viewer by second 8." |
| **The specific data point** | "The #1 creative failure in [category] right now isn't the hook. It's the reveal timing." |
| **The call-out** | "If you're spending $X/day on [category] ads and not pre-testing creative, here's what's likely happening." |

Never start a post with "I" as the first word (LinkedIn algorithm deprioritizes it).
Never start with a question (lower engagement than statements).
Never start with a preamble ("In today's post I want to talk about...").

## ENGAGEMENT RULES

- Reply to every comment within 2 hours of posting
- End replies with a question to keep thread alive
- When someone asks "how does this work?" — give them one specific thing in the reply, then offer to DM
- When an agency founder engages — DM them within 24 hours: "Saw you engaged with the [hook friction] post. We've been auditing a lot of [their category] creative lately. Happy to show you what it looks like on one of your current ads if useful."
