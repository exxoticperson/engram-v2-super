# ENGRAM Outreach Skill

This skill builds targeted lead lists and writes outreach for ENGRAM — a pre-launch creative diagnostics service for performance creative agencies managing Meta/TikTok ads for DTC/ecommerce brands.

---

## Target ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Primary:**
Performance creative agencies managing Meta/TikTok paid social for DTC/ecom brands with $30K–$500K/month ad spend across their client base.

**Why agencies over brands:**
- One sale = 20+ client accounts = recurring volume
- They already buy creative analytics tools (Motion, TripleWhale, Northbeam)
- They speak performance language fluently — faster sales cycle
- They accumulate more ad creative = faster dataset growth for ENGRAM

**Signals that qualify a prospect:**
- Agency describes itself as "performance creative," "paid social," "DTC growth," "Meta ads," "creative strategy"
- Posts about creative testing, hook rates, ad fatigue, creative iteration
- Uses Motion, MagicBrief, AdSpy, or similar tools (means they buy creative intelligence)
- Has 5–50 employees (small enough to move fast, big enough to have real spend)
- Posts case studies showing ROAS, hook rate, or hold rate language

**Signals that disqualify:**
- Pure media buying (no creative involvement)
- Brand-only focus (no performance component)
- Enterprise-only clients (procurement will kill the sale)
- Below 5 employees (probably can't pay retainer rates)

## Outreach Messages

### LinkedIn DM (Primary Channel)

**Rules:**
- Under 100 words
- No jargon (no "neural," no "fMRI," no "brain AI")
- One specific observation about their work
- One clear value prop
- One low-friction ask

**Template A — Pain-first:**
```
Hey [Name] — noticed you posted about [specific thing: hook rates / creative fatigue / testing volume].

We run pre-launch diagnostics on short-form video ads — we flag exactly where an ad loses the viewer and why, before you spend media budget on it.

Thinking it could be useful for [agency name]'s workflow. Would you want to see what it looks like on one of your current creatives?
```

**Template B — Tool-awareness:**
```
Hey [Name] — saw you're using [Motion/TripleWhale/etc.] for creative analytics.

We do the pre-launch side — before an ad goes live, we diagnose where it loses the viewer and hand you the specific recut.

Worth a quick look? Happy to run it on one of your current ads, no cost.
```

**Template C — Category insight:**
```
Hey [Name] — been auditing a lot of [DTC beauty/fitness/fashion] ads lately.

Seeing a consistent pattern: [specific insight e.g., "product reveals after 8s are killing hold rate across the category"].

We do pre-launch creative diagnostics — flag where an ad breaks before media spend. Would a quick sample audit on one of your creatives be useful?
```

## Sales Call Framework

When you get a call, follow this structure:

**First 10 minutes — discovery:**
- "Walk me through how you currently decide which ad to push budget behind."
- "What does your creative testing process look like before launch?"
- "What's the biggest creative failure you've had in the last 3 months?"
- "Do you have a way to predict performance before spending media?"

Listen for: budget wasted on bad creative, gut-feel decisions, post-hoc reporting only, client pressure to know which ad to run.

**Middle 10 minutes — positioning:**
- Show the sample audit
- Walk through one real ad diagnosis (hook friction, reveal timing, CTA gap)
- Explain the recut brief output
- Show the ENGRAM score breakdown

Do NOT pitch the tech stack. Do NOT mention TRIBE, brain data, neural networks.

**Last 10 minutes — close:**
- "Does this solve a real problem for your team right now?"
- "Which of your current clients would get the most value from knowing this before launch?"
- Offer: "Let's do a pilot audit on one of your real ads — [price]. If you don't find value, that's the end of it."

## Pilot Offer

First paid engagement should be positioned as:

**ENGRAM Pilot Audit**
- 1–3 ads audited
- Full Pre-Flight report per ad
- 30-min debrief call to walk through findings
- Price: $2,500–$3,500

This is low enough to get a yes without procurement. High enough to signal you're not cheap freelance work.
