## PAGE 7 — REPAIR RECOMMENDATIONS

### WHAT TO FIX

---

**Issue 1 — Reveal Waste (HIGH) at 0:11**

**Diagnosis:**
The product appears 11 seconds into a 30-second ad. In the beauty/skincare category this week, ads revealing the product after the 8-second mark are showing below-average hold rates. The audience that clicked through from a problem-oriented hook needs a product signal by second 5–6 to understand what they're watching and why they should stay.

**Fix:**
Insert a 1–2 second product shot between 0:04 and 0:06 — this can be a close-up, pack shot, or texture reveal. It does not need to be a full product explanation. It needs to signal "this is what solves what I just showed you." The reveal at 0:11 stays — you're adding an earlier product signal, not replacing the reveal.

**Expected impact:**
Predicted improvement in hold rate through Zone 2. The drift period between hook and reveal is currently a filter — viewers who don't see a product anchor are leaving. Bridging this extends predicted attention through to the full value proposition.

---

**Issue 2 — Hook Friction (HIGH) at 0:00–0:03**

**Diagnosis:**
The first frame is visually compelling — it creates a genuine pattern interrupt. But there is no verbal or written anchor explaining what the viewer is watching or why they should care. In this category, the hook needs to do two things simultaneously: grab attention AND orient the viewer. This hook does the first but not the second.

**Fix:**
Add one line of on-screen copy in the first 2 seconds. It should name the problem the viewer has, not describe the product. Examples in the hook variant section below. Keep the visual as-is — only add the copy layer.

**Expected impact:**
Reduces the disorientation window that currently causes early exit. A viewer who understands within 2 seconds what problem is being addressed is more likely to watch through to the reveal and CTA.

---

**Issue 3 — Comprehension Drift (HIGH) at 0:04–0:08**

**Diagnosis:**
The four-second window between the hook and the product signal is currently empty of new information. The viewer's brain is processing the visual but receiving no narrative advance. This is the gap that most frequently causes scroll-aways in short-form — momentum stalls and the scroll reflex takes over.

**Fix:**
Either: (a) add a second shot or text overlay between 0:04–0:06 that advances the problem statement or introduces social proof, OR (b) implement the product signal from Issue 1 fix here — a product flash doubles as a comprehension anchor. These two fixes can be solved with a single edit decision.

**Expected impact:**
Closes the Dead Air Window simultaneously. Reduces the most significant exit point in the current cut.

### 3 Hook Variants (Copy to test on existing footage)

**Hook A:** *(Problem-direct)*
`"If [specific skin concern] won't go away, this is why."`
— Names the problem in the viewer's language. Creates immediate identification. Strong for cold traffic.

**Hook B:** *(Curiosity-first)*
`"The reason your [routine/product/result] isn't working — and what actually does."`
— Opens a knowledge gap. Works well for retargeting or warm audiences who've seen the category.

**Hook C:** *(Social proof-led)*
`"[X,000] people switched from [common alternative] to this. Here's what changed."`
— Numbers-first credibility. Works for audiences skeptical of beauty claims. Requires accuracy.
