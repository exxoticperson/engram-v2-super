# ENGRAM Sample Audit Template
## Compact live baseline for first-client validation

This is the current report structure to sell and deliver with now. Keep it compact, sharp, and easy to forward.

The live proof asset is:

- `reports/engram_client_answer_compact.html`

Use this template to populate the content inside that format.

## Section 1: Cover and Verdict

- Brand
- Platform
- Category
- Date
- Verdict: GO / CONDITIONAL GO / NO GO
- One-line conclusion

Example:

> The ad introduces the product. It never earns attention.

## Section 2: Attention Signature

Show only the high-value reading:

- attention curve
- threshold line
- first major drop
- risk zones
- best moment
- weakest moment

Keep labels simple and timestamped.

## Section 3: Performance Breakdown

Use four client-readable scores only:

- Hook
- Hold
- Reveal
- Close

For each:

- score or rating
- one hard conclusion

Do not expose internal metric language here.

## Section 4: Breakpoints

Limit to the top three moments that matter.

For each breakpoint:

- timestamp
- what fails
- why it matters

## Section 5: Fixes

Limit to the top three fixes worth testing.

For each fix:

- what to change
- where to change it
- expected outcome

## Section 6: Launch Recommendation

- final recommendation
- what can stay
- what must change before launch
- one-sentence next step
- proof boundary

## Confidence labels

- HIGH: final cut plus context or performance data
- MEDIUM: final cut without supporting data
- LOW: rough cut or partial context
- CONTEXT-LIMITED: description only

## Required proof boundary

> ENGRAM diagnostics identify likely attention failures and comprehension gaps before spend. They are a decision-support tool designed to improve launch choices and reduce creative waste. They are not guarantees of conversion rate, ROAS, or sales.
