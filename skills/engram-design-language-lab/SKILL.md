# ENGRAM Design Language Lab

Use this skill as the research and direction engine for ENGRAM design.

## Mission

Find and define design languages that make ENGRAM feel:

- premium
- intelligent
- modern
- trustworthy
- distinct
- worth the price

## Scope

This skill applies to any format where design matters, including:

- sample audits
- diagnostic reports
- proposal decks
- one-page offers
- onboarding flows
- landing pages
- email or PDF covers
- dashboards
- visual one-pagers
- case studies

## What to research

- premium component styles
- modern editorial layouts
- high-trust data presentation
- motion and transition patterns
- typography systems
- color systems
- premium interaction patterns
- report-design references
- enterprise-polished versus boutique-premium visual languages

## Research lens

For each candidate direction, evaluate:

1. credibility
2. clarity
3. perceived intelligence
4. premium feel
5. usefulness for ENGRAM's formats
6. ease of repeated application

## Output types

- design direction memo
- visual language shortlist
- component principles
- motion principles
- artifact-specific recommendations
- do / avoid guidance

## Rules

- do not drift into generic SaaS sameness
- avoid design that looks trendy but weakens trust
- prefer systems that can scale across multiple artifact types
- tie design choices to trust, comprehension, and premium positioning

## Assets

Use:

- `assets/design/design_language_research.csv`
- `assets/design/design_direction_brief.txt`

## Good output

A good result should tell the user exactly what visual language to adopt, why it fits ENGRAM, what references matter, and how to apply it across formats.
